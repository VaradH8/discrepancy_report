What happens to your drawings, step by step.

The batch run performs three stages on every paired sheet — compare, cross-reference, register filter. This page only explains them; nothing here runs any processing.

pair sheets → 01 compare → 02 cross-reference → 03 register filter → zip of .xlsx reports
00

Pair the sheets

Every drawing in the issued folder is matched to its counterpart in the new-revision folder by the sheet number in the filename — SHT-01, SHT01, …-01_Rev X and SHEET 1 styles are all recognised. Sheets that exist in only one folder are skipped and listed as a warning.

01

Compare — what changed on this sheet?

Both drawings are converted server-side (LibreDWG / ODA, DWG → DXF) and scanned for equipment tags — modelspace plus every block definition, including tags nested inside block references — along with their datum positions.

The two tag-sets are diffed: each piece of equipment comes out as ADDED (only in the new revision), REMOVED (only in the issued drawing), or PRESENT IN BOTH. Where positions are available, movement between revisions is computed too.

02

Cross-reference — is the change unique, or drawn elsewhere?

Every added / removed tag from step 01 is then searched across all the other sheets of the issued set. The verdict decides the label:

GLOBALLY ADDED / REMOVED — the tag is found on no other sheet: a genuinely new (or fully removed) piece of equipment.
LOCALLY ADDED / REMOVED — the tag still exists on other sheets: the change is local to this sheet only.

03

Register filter — keep only registered equipment

Rows are kept only if the equipment name appears in the master register: the yellow-highlighted subset of the EquipmentName column in Equipment List to Inventive 1.xlsx. Those ~900 names are pre-extracted from that workbook and bundled with the server (equipment_registered.txt), so nothing needs uploading — every registered report is built against it automatically.

If the register Excel changes, re-run tools/extract_register.py against the updated workbook to refresh the bundled list.

→

Output

One zip containing, per paired sheet, SHT-XX_Registered.xlsx (register-filtered, statuses rewritten to GLOBALLY / LOCALLY ADDED / REMOVED) and SHT-XX_Changes.xlsx (the same diff, unfiltered) — the files used to drive revision clouding.

Ready? Everything above runs automatically — just drop your two folders on the Batch folders tab and hit Run.
